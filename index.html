Build

I help build tech and teams to improve manufacturing with software and data.

Most recently, I've been US Operations Lead at Pashi, the operating system for manufacturing. This being a seed-stage YC startup, I wear multiple hats -- and working in this environment has helped me act faster, write better and think critically. With product marketing, for example, I set up in a few days and then hosted Pashi's podcast, the Means of Production, to speak to manufacturing experts on their factory floor challenges. Wearing the product management hat I authored exhaustive documentation for features, stress tested the platform, built data models, designed key operational metrics, and mocked detailed front-end mockups for new and existing features closely working with the development and UI teams. As the voice of the manufacturing customer, I ensured the rest of the company understood what key stakeholders in factories, like process engineers, production supervisors, plant managers and quality and maintenance teams need on a daily basis.

Before that I was at Ford Motor Company for 6 years. There, I translated my experience of building vast and complex assembly lines (for powertains that go into Ford's marquee products) to create data engineering assets to help production managers make better on-floor decisions. I also served part-time as co-owner for an app I ideated, pitched and funded with C-suite executives to digitize launch-related activities on the factory floor.

I like being at the intersection of manufacturing and software and I believe that the truth is on the factory floor; we just need the right teams and tools to find it and realize a lot of potential for improvement.
