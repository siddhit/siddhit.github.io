Here are the books I enjoyed the most. All links are from Amazon (and I've chosen the Kindle versions) and I don't get any commission.

Indian Stories

The books that got me started on reading are simple to list:

Amar Chitra Katha, by Anant Pai

For being the reading staple for so many kids in India, Anant Pai, or "Uncle Pai" was like Disney but better since these comics weren't animation but still graphic novels and I could make the jump to actual books and of course, taught great values.


The Mahabharat, by Ved Vyas

For being the mother of all stories and 'the longest poem ever written' and which drives most of my philosophy even today. It's, of course, full of things that could be challenged with science but I don't know why the same rigor isn't applied to the Illiad, Odyssey, Journey to the West or any other Abrahamic story. Its plain acceptance that life is suffering, that detachment alone will give you peace, complexity of emotions, turn of events, and above all, its chief message of complete surrender to one of four paths to the truth from its core - the Bhagavad Gita (which I strive to read) - has ensured its popularity and value persisted longer than any fiction or non-fiction book there is.

Several Gujarati stories by Gaurishankar 'Dhumketu' Joshi (whose work I translated and adapted here), and Chunnilal Madiya.

For their sublime and pained portrayal of real life in a poverty-stricken era of India, teaching me what little I know about Gujarati literature and exposing me to the more intellectual side of a very unartistic and commercial community of people.

Fiction

These are fiction books not of Indian origin. I'm listing fiction first with science-fiction alternately with non-science-fiction. I've been told non-fiction is where the real wisdom is. But fiction is what got me into reading and the way I see it is that every fiction book is just a colorful visage wrapped around a core of real first-hand learnings of the author. Plus, I've read that you should read what you love until you love to read and I'll say that to everyone I can. For non-fiction, jump to it here.

The Ultimate Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy, by Douglas Adams

For the best combination of wisdom with nonsense and satire with silliness I have seen in a science fiction book.


To Kill a Mockingbirgd, by Harper Lee

For a story of empathy in which events of terrible injustice are seen through the gentle eyes of a child and her relationship with her father.


The Fountainhead, by Ayn Rand

For its somewhat controversial philosophy of objectivism but also for a wonderful account of the career trajectories of two frenemies and the evolution of their competence.


Foundation, by Isaac Asimov

For showing how decision making can last not just a few decades but across planet systems and millenia. And also for inspiring Elon Musk.

Non-fiction

These are books that I see more as directives and concepts I need to understand than worlds I need to imagine. I've listed them in terms of how much I remember them, alternating between books that help me go closer to my truth and others. To jump back to fiction recommendations, click here.

The Power of Now, by Echkart Tolle

For the best conversation-style book on why it's futile to live in anything but the present, the now. I listened to this book on Audible and I don't regret not reading it because I remember Eckart's gentle voice much better than I would have remembered myself reading it.


Benjamin Franklin: An American Life, by Walter Isaacson

For bringing a more approachable version of America's Renaissance man, rags-to-riches story and possibly the coolest American Founding Father there was. I frankly learned more about American history from this than what I learned in world history subjects in high school and popular media. Again, so much better on Audible since it had an excellent narrator switching between voicing Franklin and the story.


The Goal, by Eliyahu Goldratt

For the manufacturing guy in me. A way to see solve problems by identifying bottlenecks and applying small, common-sense improvements with clarity.
